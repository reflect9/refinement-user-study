Understanding and Improving News Topics
We are researchers from the University of Maryland attempting to measure how people understand themes discovered from newspaper articles, and suggest improvements. Participants of the study are required to be at least 18 years old. If you have any questions about this study, please contact Leah Findlater (http://terpconnect.umd.edu/~leahkf/). Press the button below if you voluntarily agree to participate.

Imagine that you have thousands of New York Times articles published in January 2007, and have to create themes that summarize what the articles are about. While the task seems tedious and time consuming, computers can help.

This screen shows one of the themes that have been automatically identified by the computer, and the news articles that go with that theme.
1. Read over the theme. The computer has grouped the words that appear together in many news articles. The sizes and orders of words represent how frequently they appear in the associated articles.
Question. What word appears most frequently in the theme?
2. Explore the theme and articles. Click a few articles to see full text. Hover the mouse cursor over words to see some context for where it appears in the articles.
Question. What is the title of the first article containing the theme word "ballet"?
