Understanding and Improving Overview of News
We are researchers from the University of Maryland creating new technologies to generate overviews of news articles. In this study, we are interested in how people understand and improve automatically generated overviews. Participants are required to be at least 18 years old. If you have any questions about the study, please contact Leah Findlater (http://terpconnect.umd.edu/~leahkf/). Press the button below if you voluntarily agree to participate.

Imagine that you have thousands of New York Times articles published in January 2007. Your task is to create themes that summarize what they articles are about. Although the task may seem tedious and time consuming, a computer program can help you get started.
This screen shows one of the themes that have been automatically identified by the computer program and the news articles that go with that theme.
1. Read over the theme. The computer has grouped the words that appear together in many news articles. The sizes and orders of words represent how frequently they appear in the associated articles.
Question. What word appears most frequently in the Theme?
2. Explore the theme and articles. Click a few articles to see full text. Hover the mouse cursor over the theme words to see some context for where they appear in the articles.
Question. What is the title of the first article containing the theme word "ballet"?
Through this HIT, you will see three themes. For each theme, you will be asked to answer questions about how well you understand the theme, as well as ideas for improving them. Participants who were best at improving themes will receive $10 bonus.

Completing the entire HIT will take around 20 minutes. Please read questions carefully and answer every question.
