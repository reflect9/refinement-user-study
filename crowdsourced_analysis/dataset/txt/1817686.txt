metro briefing | new jersey: paterson: reward offered in officer's shooting   the paterson police said yesterday that a reward for information about a man who fatally shot an off-duty police officer sunday morning has been increased to more than $40,000 from an initial $1,000. the officer, tyron d. franklin, 23, was killed shortly after 1 a.m. in what the police said was a holdup at broadway fried chicken. the extra money is being offered by a combination of foundations and private donors, the police said. detectives have interviewed several witnesses and the police said yesterday that the shooting might be linked to a surge in robberies.  nate schweber
