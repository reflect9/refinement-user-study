seton hall starts big east play on right track, beating rutgers   for now, seton hall looks like the best big east basketball team from the state of new jersey. for the pirates, that may be as good as it gets the rest of this season.  in front of a small crowd (7,214) at continental arena that included an impartial gov. jon s. corzine, seton hall defeated interstate rival rutgers, 77-72, wednesday night.  the game kicked off the big east portion of the schedule for seton hall and rutgers, which figures to mean problems for both. neither team fared particularly well in the nonconference portion of their schedules and must now play much tougher opponents.  both have first-year coaches and are in a rebuilding mode. bobby gonzalez has come over from manhattan to try to get seton hall (9-4, 1-0) back on track. fred hill is the new coach at rutgers (7-7, 0-1).  so far, it is gonzalez's team that seems to be on the faster track.  ''we don't know how long we are going to stay undefeated in the league, but the bottom line is that we're 9-4 and we won our first big east game, and it's a rivalry game,'' gonzalez said. ''rivalry games are important. we've got to find ways to win games and scratch and claw. that's what we did. that's what we're going to have to do every game. no games this year are going to be easy for us.''  the pirates have a talented freshman, eugene harvey, they can build around. a point guard, he had 21 points for seton hall on wednesday. brian laing also had 21.  ''eugene harvey makes everyone else on the floor better,'' hill said. ''everybody gets worried about eugene's penetration and that opens up the floor for laing and jamar nutter. he's their catalyst and he makes them go. the better eugene plays and as he continues to develop and grow, they will develop and grow as a team.''  it appeared early on that seton hall was going to run away with the game. the pirates had a 34-16 lead with 5 minutes 17 seconds to go in the first half.  but the scarlet knights went on a 17-0 run to cut the pirates' lead to 34-33. rutgers had its first lead, 54-53, on a hook shot by j. r. inman with 10:11 to go. seton hall then started to take control. when harvey scored on a driving layup with 4:44 to play, the pirates had a 68-61 lead.  rutgers was not done. when hamaday n'diaye dunked with 1:07 left to go, the seton hall lead was down to 68-66. making 9 of 10 free-throw attempts inside the final minute, seton hall was able to close out the game.  rutgers was led by inman, who had 18 points and 12 rebounds.  st. john's 64,
