house smoking ban   to the editor:  re ''house bans smoking, and few complain'' (news article, jan. 11):  the new leadership's ban on smoking inside the house of representatives was more than a symbolic gesture indicating that ''the days of smoke-filled rooms in the capitol are over.''  it was also a concrete action strengthening one of the most important tools recognized by public health to prevent addiction to tobacco -- and that is to make smoking socially unacceptable.  smoking is the largest preventable cause of premature death in the united states (responsible for 1 of every 5 deaths), killing some 438,000 americans annually. with help from congress, we can end the days of smoke-filled rooms everywhere in america.  barry r. bloom  boston, jan. 11, 2007  the writer is dean of the harvard school of public health.
