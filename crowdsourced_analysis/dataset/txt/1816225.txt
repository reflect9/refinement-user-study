physical culture; a pocketful of better skiing   only the most devoted treat their skis and snowboards to a professional hot wax as often as recommended -- every three to five outings. the process can be inconvenient and costly, so in recent years, do-it-yourselfers have started using easy-to-apply waxes and glide-enhancing solutions.  these products usually contain a fluorinated additive that helps repel water and decrease friction, making it easier to control speed and maneuver. for the finicky enthusiast, most of them are marketed for specific conditions: a thinner, slicker formula for wet, flat runs, or thicker wax to protect gear from dry snow.  some waxes come off almost as easily as they go on, so it's best to apply them throughout the day on the mountain. ''in about two minutes you can make controlling your speed and motion easier,'' said brad nelson, the chairman of the national ski and snowboard retailers association. ''why not do something so simple that can make an o.k. day of skiing a great one?''  jessica cassity
