metro briefing | new york: manhattan: body found in central park   a city parks department employee discovered a man's body yesterday morning near the ramble and the 77th street pedestrian entrance to central park, the police said. the dead man, donald bond, 43, was dressed in blue jeans, cowboy boots and an argyle sweater and was lying face down on a rocky slope leading to a small creek. he had no visible signs of trauma and nothing appeared to have been stolen from him, the police said. mr. bond, who lived on third avenue and 102nd street, was found about 8 a.m. and was believed to have died earlier in the morning, the police said.  emily vasquez
