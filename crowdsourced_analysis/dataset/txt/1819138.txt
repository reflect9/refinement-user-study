hiring the headhunter   governor spitzer named more than 300 people to his transition team. but he also hired some outside professionals to help him build an administration: isaacson, miller, a headhunting firm. he had apparently found out firsthand that it could be effective: its web site says that it helped the natural resources defense council, an environmental group, hire peter lehner, who was the chief of mr. spitzer's environmental protection bureau when he was attorney general.  michael cooper  albany notebook
