at the market: whiting   before you pillage your purse for the fish you usually buy, consider whiting. it's an unsung treat, a local winter catch selling for $3 or so a pound.  buy the silvery-skinned fish whole, just cleaned. dip it in milk, roll it in white cornmeal mixed with flour, and fry in butter or oil. the flesh will lift off the sturdy central bone with ease. lemon wedges, tartar sauce or brown butter will dress it up.  you can figure on a largish fish (about a pound) or two smaller ones per person. whiting are $2.49 a pound at new star fish in the essex street market, $2.99 at citarella and fairway markets and $3 at pe & dd seafood at the union square greenmarket on saturdays.  food stuff
