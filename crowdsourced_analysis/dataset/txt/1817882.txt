world briefing | asia: myanmar: u.s. seeks u.n. pressure on repression   the united states introduced a security council resolution calling on the military rulers of myanmar, formerly burma, to begin a transition to democracy and release all political prisoners, including daw aung san suu kyi, the nobel peace prize-winning opposition leader. china and russia both oppose the move, but alejandro d. wolff, the acting american ambassador, said he hoped for a vote this week. mr. wolff said that the release of 2,831 prisoners announced last week had focused on petty criminals. ''if you look at the facts,'' he said. ''you'll find that there are more political prisoners today than there were a year ago.''  warren hoge
