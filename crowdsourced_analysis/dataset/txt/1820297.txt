ballet therapy   to the editor,  re ''once a power, for better or worse'' by gia kourlas [jan. 14]:  i visited charles france, then the assistant to mikhail baryshnikov, in the hospital around 1986. he was less fearsome in a patient's gown. his face lit up, then fell. ''oh, you came to talk about work.'' no, i replied, i had merely come to visit him. at his insistence we spent the next hour talking about nothing but the ballet, his great love, and his mood lifted. it marked the end of an adversarial relationship and the start of beneficial mentoring. what a difference.  alan olshan  manhattan  the writer was director of special projects for american ballet theater from 1980 to 1990.  charles france
