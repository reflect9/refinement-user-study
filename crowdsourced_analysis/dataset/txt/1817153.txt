investors start the new year indecisively   the year got off to an uneven start on wall street last week, with the dow jones industrial average and the broader standard & poor's 500-stock index both dropping modestly. but the nasdaq composite index, in which technology stocks dominate, eked out a small gain.  many analysts expect technology earnings to be strong this year. on thursday, after banc of america securities issued a bullish earnings projection for intel, the chip maker, tech shares rose sharply. but on friday, the sector's shares dropped on a disappointing earnings report by motorola, the cellphone maker, whose stock had a one-day loss of 7.8 percent.  for the week, intel had the best performance in the dow, with a gain of 4.2 percent. goodyear tire's gain of 10.9 percent led the s.& p. 500. the company said it had reached an agreement with striking workers that would enable it to cut costs.  for the week, the dow lost 65.14 points, or 0.5 percent, to close at 12,398.01. the s.& p. 500 lost 8.59 points, or 0.6 percent, to close at 1,409.71. the nasdaq composite index rose 18.96 points, or 0.8 percent, to close at 2,434.25.  the yield on the benchmark 10-year treasury note slipped to 4.64 percent from 4.70 percent the previous week.  jeff sommer  databank
