health care and rehabilitation deeply mourn the passing of our beloved past chairman of the board and friend, jack parker. we extend our most heartfelt condolences to norma parker & the entire parker family. jack parker's life and accomplishments are measured by superlatives only. a cherished husband, father, grandfather and great grandfather, & founder of the jack parker corporation, jack was a dominant, highly respected figure in the building industry for many decades. internationally known for his building and ownership of le parker meridien hotel, he was responsible for a remarkable amount of real estate development throughout the east coast of the united states & beyond. together with his loving wife, norma, the parkers have been active leaders at the institute since 1967, when mr. parker became a member of the board of trustees. he served as chairman of the building committee, and was responsible for the construction of the facility that opened its doors in new hyde park in 1972. he was appointed chairman of the board (1972 -1975) and, in recognition of the great generosity of the parker family, the institute was named ''parker jewish geriatric institute'' in 1988. the institute's name was later revised to more accurately reflect its renowned specialization in both health care and rehabilitation, and its expanding role in providing care for adults of all ages, a vision exemplary of jack parker's steadfast commitment to improve the quality of life. jack parker will be missed as a friend and as a leader, but the memory of jack parker will forever burn bright as a shining example of how much good can be done by an individual who possesses a keen awareness of humanitarian issues and a profound commitment to philanthropic causes. jerry landsberg, chairman of
