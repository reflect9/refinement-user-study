spare times: jan. 12 - jan. 18; for children   'adventures from ezra jack keats: apt. 3 and maggie and the pirate' (tonight through monday) two books by this author and illustrator come to life onstage in the winter musical from tada!, the youth theater troupe. ''apt. 3'' focuses on the friendship that two children forge with a blind neighbor; ''maggie and the pirate'' is about, well, a girl and a pirate (a small friendly one). (through feb. 11.) tonight at 7, tomorrow and sunday at 2 and 4 p.m., monday at noon and 2 p.m., tada!, 15 west 28th street, chelsea, (212) 252-1619; $18; $8 for ages 15 and under.  annual library tribute to dr. martin luther king jr. (tomorrow) king inspired more than his own generation, and this yearly celebration at the central library in queens encourages children's participation in his legacy. the tribute, from 12:30 to 4:30 p.m., features a screening of the documentary ''martin luther king jr.: a man and his dream,'' followed by a discussion; poetry, dance and music performances; and a reception for the students, ages 6 to 18, who wrote letters to king as part of an annual writing project at the library. at 89-11 merrick boulevard, jamaica, (718) 990-0767; free.  'arthur' exhibition (tomorrow through next friday) arthur, the lovable cartoon aardvark who stars in a series of books and in his own pbs show, has always made children feel better about their lives. but now, as a result of an auction to take place at sotheby's on jan. 21, he will contribute to their physical health as well. marc brown, arthur's creator, has donated 30 original arthur drawings whose sale (part of a larger americana auction) will benefit children's cancer care at memorial sloan kettering cancer center. the drawings are on display, free, at sotheby's, 1334 york avenue, at 72nd street, (212) 606-7000, sothebys.com. hours: tomorrow and monday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; sunday, 1 to 5 p.m.; tuesday through thursday, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m.  audrarox (tomorrow) one way to get children to listen to parents: put mom and dad in a rock band. most of the members of audrarox, which sounds a bit like the go-go's, have children themselves. the group, founded by audra tsanos, will play at 11 a.m. at the leonard nimoy thalia at symphony space, 2537 broadway, at 95th street, (212) 864-5400, symphonyspace.org. tickets: in advance, $15 and $10 for ages 12 and under; at the door, $18 and $13.  'brain games' visitors 5 and older can explore the worlds inside their own heads in this new exhibition, opening tomorrow at the children's museum of manhattan. displays include a brain map, medical scans and, most important, 36 interactive puzzles to exercise that all-important organ. (through mid-april.) open tuesdays through sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., the tisch building, 212 west 83rd street, (212) 721-1223, cmom.org; $9; $6 for 65+.  'cenicienta' ('cinderella') (sunday) the heroine could easily lose her glass slipper dancing the tango in this latin-flavored musical from teatro sea, presented in an easily understood combination of english and spanish. at 3 p.m. at thalia spanish theater, 41-17 greenpoint avenue, sunnyside, queens, (718) 729-3880, thalilatheatre.org; $12; $10 for children.  funkey monkeys (sunday) these aren't zoo residents but a band formed by joshua sitron, composer and musical director for the nickelodeon show ''dora the explorer.'' the members do comic bits along with their music, which is funky of course. at 2 p.m., the jewish museum, 1109 fifth avenue, at 92nd street, (212) 423-3337; $12; $10 for museum family members.  'grow involved' (monday) this annual event, run by the nonprofit group children for children, honors the memory of the rev. dr. martin luther king jr. by putting his philosophy into action. from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m., young people can participate in public-service projects, from making birthday cards for the elderly to knitting pieces for baby blankets. this year it is at two manhattan locations: martin luther king jr. high school, 122 amsterdam avenue, at 65th street, and p.s./i.s. 57, james weldon johnson school, 176 east 115th street; free. (children must be accompanied by an adult.) information: (212) 850-4172, childrenforchildren.org.
