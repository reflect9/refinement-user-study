at last, red sox will sign drew   the boston red sox and outfielder j. d. drew have resolved issues surrounding his five-year, $70 million contract, and drew's signing is expected today, the boston globe reported yesterday.  more than seven weeks after drew's agent, scott boras, announced that drew had reached a contract agreement with boston, the red sox have gained the ability to void up to the final two years of the contract should a pre-existing condition in drew's shoulder force him to go on the disabled list, according to the globe.  the newspaper said that red sox doctors detected potential indicators that drew could develop shoulder problems in three to five years. drew had shoulder surgery while with the los angeles dodgers.
