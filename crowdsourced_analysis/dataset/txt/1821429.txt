spare times: jan 26-feb. 1; for children   'adventures from ezra jack keats: apt. 3 and maggie and the pirate' (tomorrow and sunday) two books by this author and illustrator come to life onstage in the winter musical from tada!, the youth theater troupe. ''apt. 3'' focuses on the friendship two children forge with a blind neighbor; ''maggie and the pirate'' is about, well, a girl and a pirate (a small, friendly one). (through feb. 11.) at 2 and 4 p.m., tada!, 15 west 28th street, chelsea, (212) 252-1619, tadatheater.com; $18; $8 for ages 17 and under.  'the 5,000 fingers of dr. t' (tomorrow) most children are fondly acquainted with dr. seuss the author, but dr. seuss the auteur? he did create this one film, a live-action 88-minute movie musical about a boy and his merciless music teacher, made in 1953. at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m., the leonard nimoy thalia, symphony space, 2537 broadway, at 95th street, (212) 864-5400, symphonyspace.org; $10; $8 for ages 12 and under; $6 for members of the just kidding club.  'gustafer yellowgold's wide wild world'  (tomorrow) another space alien has landed, but he's not interested in colonizing the planet. hailing from the sun and described as part feline, gustafer yellowgold is the creation of the illustrator and composer morgan taylor, who will present gustafer's tales with live music, slides and narration. at 2 p.m., museum of the moving image, 35th avenue at 36th street, astoria, queens, (718) 784-0077, movingimage.us; $10; $6 for members; free for toddlers on laps (price includes museum admission).  susie ibarra and roberto rodriguez: 'mundo niÑos' (sunday) percussionists and composers, ms. ibarra and mr. rodriguez use their interactive world music show to teach vocabulary and counting in english and spanish. ''mundo niños'' (''children's world'') features not only latin influences, but also asian, african and american. at noon, joe's pub, 425 lafayette street, at astor place, east village, (212) 967-7555, joespub.com; $15; $10 for under 12; a $12 minimum at tables. table reservations: (212) 539-8778.  just a matter of time: the musical (tonight through sunday; tuesday and wednesday) eleven-year-old meg is trying to do what a lot of us strive to do: find time. but in this show by sandra dutton (book and lyrics) and jack kohl (music), time is an actual character, and as meg pursues him through a maze, she encounters a lot of interesting creatures. sage theater, 711 seventh avenue, near 47th street, manhattan, (212) 868-4444; $18. tonight at 8; tomorrow at 2 (sold out) and 8 p.m.; sunday at 3 p.m.; tuesday at 7 p.m.; wednesday at 2 and 8 p.m.  'mother goose!' (tomorrow and sunday) remember the old woman who lived in a shoe and had so many children she didn't know what to do? this weekend she has an appropriate solution: she will have all her sons and daughters dance. they're the stars of new york theater ballet's ''mother goose!,'' an hourlong adaptation of nursery rhymes. at 11 a.m. and 1 and 3:30 p.m., gould hall, 55 east 59th street, manhattan, (212) 355-6160, nytb.org; $30; $25 for ages 12 and under.  'the new kid' (tomorrow) often the also-ran in the race for popularity, ''the new kid'' usually has a tough time adjusting to school. in this hourlong musical by paul armento, a boy named zack copes with the challenges of fitting in while still staying true to his values. at 11 a.m., st. peter's lutheran church, lexington avenue at 54th street, (212) 352-3101; $22.  'pinkalicious' (tomorrow and sunday) sometimes being in the pink can be too much of a good thing, as the pink-obsessed title character finds out in this new musical from vital children's theater, which elizabeth and victoria kann adapted from their children's book. tomorrow and sunday at 11 a.m. and 1 p.m., 2162 broadway, at 76th street, fourth floor, (212) 352-3101; $18.
