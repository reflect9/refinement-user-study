metro briefing | new york: queens: shortcut through airport screening   for the first time in the new york area, starting today some passengers will be able to pay to bypass an airport security screening line through a program that has come to kennedy international airport. but for now, they will still have to take off their shoes. clear registered traveler, a private service that prescreens travelers for a $100 annual fee, will open a kiosk at terminal 7 today, making kennedy the second american airport -- after orlando international airport in florida -- to provide an express lane through security, said steven brill, the chief executive of clear. the kiosk at kennedy is equipped with a device that scans travelers' shoes on their feet, but so far the transportation security administration has not given final approval for its use at kennedy, mr. brill said.  patrick mcgeehan
