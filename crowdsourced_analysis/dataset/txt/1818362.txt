metro briefing | connecticut: greenwich: concession dispute over   the operators of the coffee concession at the old greenwich train station have reached an agreement with town officials in which they will vacate that stand in exchange for an assurance that the town would try to help them keep their other stand at the riverside station, officials said yesterday. the operators, greg and mary maher, a married couple who have had the old greenwich stand for some years, were told in december that they had to leave after adam fairbanks, a 23-year-old greenwich man who is blind, invoked laws giving the blind preference at running government-owned concessions.  alison leigh cowan
