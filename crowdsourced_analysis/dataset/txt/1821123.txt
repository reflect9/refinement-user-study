world briefing | europe: poles and czechs to talk with u.s. about missile defense system   the united states will start formal negotiations this year with poland and the czech republic on deploying ground-based antimissile defense systems in the countries, government officials said. the decision was made despite weak public support in each country and strong opposition from russia. the united states says the system is intended as protection against a possible attack from the middle east, not russia.  judy dempsey
