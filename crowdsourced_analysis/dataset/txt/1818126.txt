webb to run mile in manhattan   alan webb, who in 2001 became the only high school miler to run faster than four minutes indoors, will return jan. 20 to the armory track and field center in manhattan, where he achieved that feat.  webb's record time of 3 minutes 59.86 seconds came in the new balance games. he will run again in that meet in the men's elite mile against james thie of wales, adrian blincoe of new zealand, andy badderly of england and jason lunn of redwood city, calif.  carmen douma-hussar of canada, who won the women's elite mile two years ago, heads the field for that race. her opposition includes sara hall of palo alto, calif.; amy mortimer of west roxbury, mass.; marina muncan of serbia and marry cullen of ireland.  frank litsky  sports briefing: track and field
