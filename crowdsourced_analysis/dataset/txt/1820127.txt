national briefing | washington: extension granted to hurricane victims   the federal government will allow the 110,000 families still living in trailers because of hurricanes katrina and rita to stay through august, a spokesman for the federal emergency management agency said. the housing program had been scheduled to expire at the end of february, and some community groups had been expecting fema to start issuing eviction notices as early as next week. the six-month extension, announced by senator mary l. landrieu, democrat of louisiana, also applies to displaced families living in apartments paid for by fema.  leslie eaton
