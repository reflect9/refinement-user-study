the right has a jailhouse conversion   it is well and good, and long overdue, to plan seriously for prisoner re-entry, but that is only half the story (chris suellentrop, dec. 24). the real problem is that we have been locking up far too many people for far too long. it's not about what we do with criminals; it's about how we define them.  when a debate emerges about whether we should imprison people whose conduct violated nobody else's rights (like most drug offenses), we'll know that real reform is stirring.  richard m. evans  northampton, mass.  of course conservatives are now interested in prison reform. so many of them (delay, ney, abramoff, cunningham, et al.) are either already there or facing the prospect.  it's no coincidence that the last time i can recall conservatives being interested in the subject was after watergate.  d. f. manno  philadelphia
