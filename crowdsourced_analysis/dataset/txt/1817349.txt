afghanistan has banned ''kabul express,'' an indian-made film dealing with the collapse of the taliban, in response to protests from the ethnic hazara minority, described in the movie as ''the most dangerous tribe'' in afghanistan, agence france-presse reported. the ministry of information and culture said on saturday that the government film directorate had prohibited the purchase, sale and showing of ''kabul express,'' even though it has yet to be released in afghanistan. but it is available in pirated copies. the 90-minute film, referring to the hazaras as murderers, rapists and thieves, tells the story of two indian journalists, covering the fall of the taliban regime in 2001, who are abducted by a pakistani military officer fighting alongside the taliban. in a statement denouncing the film, the culture ministry said it ''has scenes, dialogues and behaviors which are insulting to a tribe and in fact to all our nation.'' it said camera art, the company that made the film, and the director, kabir khan, had apologized and agreed to ''edit out irritating and insulting scenes.''
