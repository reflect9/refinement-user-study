corrections   an article and a picture caption in business day on dec. 29 about an effort to persuade farmers to grow a low-linolenic soybean, which produces cooking oil with reduced or no trans fat, referred imprecisely to the qualities of the soybean. oil from the low-linolenic soybeans has many of the same characteristics of regular cooking oil with less need for hydrogenation, a process that creates trans fat. soybeans themselves do not contain trans fat.
