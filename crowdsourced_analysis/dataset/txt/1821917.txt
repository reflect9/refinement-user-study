the making, and unmaking, of a child soldier   when my daughter was 10 years old, she used to put books that frightened her in the linen closet, under a pile of sheets. i wish it were possible for me, as an adult, to rid myself as easily of the images in ishmael beah's article (jan. 14). the dehumanizing embodied in the process of turning a child into a killer was more than i could bear.  those of us who have the good fortune to live lives relatively free of the experience of brutal cruelty would do well to practice mindful living and kindness to others at every opportunity, if only to neutralize the poisons of man's inhumanity to man.  elca branman  sarasota, fla.
