metro briefing | new york: brooklyn: council endorsement   representative yvette d. clarke, a brooklyn democrat who resigned from the city council after she was elected to congress in november, endorsed mathieu eugene yesterday to succeed her on the council. mr. eugene, a haitian immigrant who runs a youth recreation center in flatbush, faces 12 other candidates in a special election on feb. 20. at a news conference, ms. clarke's mother, una s. t. clarke, an influential former councilwoman, and the haitian-american association for progressive action, a political group, also endorsed mr. eugene. his main rivals for the seat include jesse e. hamilton iii, a democratic district leader with support from several unions; jennifer n. james, a political activist who raised money for ms. clarke's congressional race; and wellington sharpe, who runs a child care company.  sewell chan
