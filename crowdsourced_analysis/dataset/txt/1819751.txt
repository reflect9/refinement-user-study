metro briefing | new york: queens: teenager admits bias attack   a 19-year-old from auburndale has admitted attacking two asian men in douglaston last summer and beating another man with a hammer in a separate attack last may, prosecutors said yesterday. the teenager, kevin m. brown, pleaded guilty in state supreme court to assault as a hate crime for the attack on the asian men and assault for the other attack, and is scheduled to be sentenced to three and a half years in prison, said the queens district attorney, richard a. brown. prosecutors said that kevin brown, who worked as a parking valet, took part in an aug. 12 attack on john lu and reynold liang, both 19, in which he kicked and punched both men and beat mr. liang with a car steering wheel lock. on may 7, prosecutors said, he struck panagotis karatzas, 30, on the head with a hammer during a traffic dispute.
