what the teachers forget   to the editor:  though cultivating new audiences for jazz is a laudable goal, there is a growing cultural disconnect because swing has become irrelevant. by focusing solely on the music and ignoring its meaning, we are missing the larger opportunity to teach improvisation as a life skill. this was brought home to me by the late, great lester bowie. when i mentioned that most of the students in my college jazz ensemble were not music majors, lester replied, ''when you're being operated on, do you want a doctor who knows how to improvise or not?''  don glasgo  norwich, vt.  the writer is director of the barbary coast jazz ensemble at dartmouth college.  jazz education
