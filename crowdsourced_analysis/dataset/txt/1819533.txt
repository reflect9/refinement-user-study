metro briefing | new york: manhattan: milestone at ground zero center   four months after opening, the tribute w.t.c. visitor center at 120 liberty street, opposite ground zero, counted its 100,000th visitor yesterday. she was gwynnie carder, 42, of salisbury, england, who learned of the distinction while waiting in line with her husband, nigel, and two friends. speaking of the horrific event that prompted their trip to the world trade center site, mrs. carder said, ''i shouldn't have to be here.'' the nonprofit center, with five galleries devoted to 9/11, opened to the public on sept. 18. president bush visited eight days earlier and is not included in the 100,000 tally.  david w. dunlap
