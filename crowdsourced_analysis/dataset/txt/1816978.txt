what can't be measured   to the editor:  re ''music of the hemispheres'' by clive thompson [dec. 31]:  i delight in daniel levitin's research, but i am distressed by his cultural limitations. how about some experiments with a baby responding to the voice of its mother or to another child? by emphasizing timbre over tune, he mistakes the part for the whole. he might try measuring just the human voice: no instruments, no amplification. can it be that singing together is the one human activity that uses all our potential at once: physical, intellectual, emotional and spiritual? can dr. levitin measure that?  alice parker  hawley, mass.  the writer is a composer and teacher whose books include ''the anatomy of melody.''  neuroscience of music
