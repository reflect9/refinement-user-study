physical culture | gear test with paul romero, adventure racer; the edge of night   shorter daylight hours need not interfere with the comings and goings of runners, hikers and mountain bikers: a slew of new-and-improved headlamps can almost turn night into day.  in 2003, headlamp manufacturers started switching from using incandescent bulbs to high-out light-emitting diodes, or l.e.d.'s. as l.e.d. technology has improved, options have multiplied. users can choose the width of the beam their headlamp casts, how far it shines and the light intensity, said jon rockefeller, the sport division manager at petzl, a leading headlamp maker.  paul romero, left, an adventure racer for team sole, a four-person team based in big bear lake, calif., wore five headlamps during nighttime training runs, hikes and snowshoe forays in the area.  sarah bowen shea
