instrument for words, too   to the editor:  re ''the music is sweet, the words are true'' by dinitia smith [jan. 7]:  the writer deborah grace winer is quoted as saying that rosemary clooney ''was concerned about what a song said,'' and that ''she didn't use her voice as an instrument like ella fitzgerald.'' besides having a superb voice, fitzgerald was also about the lyrics, confirmed by her masterly interpretations of works by classic lyricists in her songbook series. listen closely to her sing ira gershwin's ''but not for me.'' it's restrained, achingly beautiful and full of hope. because fitzgerald consistently delivered the goods so effortlessly, we sometimes forget what a truly great singer she was.  reggie morris  los angeles  lyricists and their art
