national briefing | new england: maine: a thumbs up for beer with risque labels   the state reversed its decision to bar a massachusetts beer distributor from selling three types of beer because its labels were deemed offensive. the maine bureau of liquor enforcement said the labels, two that feature bare-breasted women and one that shows santa claus from behind and sitting on a barrel, contained ''undignified or improper illustrations.'' the distributor, shelton brothers, filed a federal lawsuit against the state, which reversed its decision dec. 20, said the owner, will shelton. he said he had not received a letter alerting him of the reversal until this week because he was in germany.  katie zezima
