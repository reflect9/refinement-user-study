samantha chapman, adam langley   samantha k. chapman, a daughter of rosemary and thomas a. chapman of fort washington, pa., was married last evening to j. adam langley, a son of deborah and larry langley of boone, n.c. the rev. linda noonan, a united methodist minister, officiated at the franklin institute in philadelphia.  both the bride, 30, and the bridegroom, 31, are postdoctoral research fellows at the smithsonian environmental research center in edgewater, md. the bride does ecological research in tropical mangrove ecosystems, and the bridegroom studies how coastal salt marshes respond to simulated future carbon dioxide levels. they met at northern arizona university, where each received a ph.d. in biology.  the bride will continue to use her name professionally. she graduated from pennsylvania state university. her father is a director of purchasing at rohm & haas, a chemical company in philadelphia. her mother conducts training and coordinates workshops on workplace safety at mcneil pharmaceuticals in fort washington.  the bridegroom graduated from north carolina state university. his parents own langley associates, a house design and construction company in boone.  weddings/celebrations
