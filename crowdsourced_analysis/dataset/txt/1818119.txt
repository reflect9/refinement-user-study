national briefing | plains: kansas: city to demolish killer's house   the mayor of a wichita suburb said the city planned to tear down the house where dennis rader, known as the b.t.k. serial killer, lived for 25 years while he carried out a string of murders. the suburb, park city, bought the house from mr. rader's wife for just under $60,000 and plans to demolish it in the next month, mayor dee stuart said. the property will be converted into an entry to an existing city park, ms. stuart said.  libby sander
