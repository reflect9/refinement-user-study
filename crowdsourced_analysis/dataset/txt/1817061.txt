rewards are great for sports specialists   to the editor:  re ''a single goal in common'' (dec. 17): financial assistance scholarships and grants from division i or ii institutions can total in excess of $30,000 per year for a deserving athlete. as such, high school athletes can ''earn'' $120,000 or more toward the cost of their college education by specializing in one sport.  this is serious money, and provides a genuine incentive for parents and kids to specialize.  the importance of diversity and the risks associated with specializing are important. but the article should have addressed the extreme financial facts of paying for college, the competition to gain admission to college, and how these factors shape the choices made by parents and their children.  andy morris  briarcliff  the writer is director of future stars sports group, armonk indoor sports center.
