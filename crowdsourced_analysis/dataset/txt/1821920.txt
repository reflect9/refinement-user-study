the right has a jailhouse conversion   it was heartening to learn from chris suellentrop's article (dec. 24) that many conservatives and liberals are rethinking whether longer prison sentences without parole, no judicial discretion in sentencing and incarceration of ever-more prisoners are really the best ways to fight crime.  i have one small comment. a symbolically important and very inexpensive way to say that people who have done wrong and are trying to do right can have a second chance is to make it easier for former prisoners to regain the right to vote.  edward witten  princeton, n.j.
