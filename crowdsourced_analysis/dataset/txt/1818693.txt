research on many fronts   to the editor:  your article on my research into music and the brain [''music of the hemispheres'' by clive thompson, dec. 31] prompted a letter writer to express her distress at the limitations of my work. i agree such research should include mothers' singing to their infants. but this requires specialized methods, and there are excellent scientists -- sandra trehub, anne fernald, laurel trainor -- pursuing just this question. they have discovered fascinating things about the human experience through parent-child musical interactions. (yes, it can be measured.)  daniel j. levitin  montreal  neuroscience of music
