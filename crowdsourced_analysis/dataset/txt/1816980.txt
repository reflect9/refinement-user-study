the familiarity factor   to the editor:  re ''if you won't play the album, they'll sing it, from the top'' [dec. 31]:  performing an album live in its entirety may, as elisabeth vincentelli suggests, be a way for an artist to stand out in a crowded marketplace, but the benefit in terms of ticket sales may have as much to do with accessibility as visibility. as acts continue to record and release music beyond their popular peak, older fans become reluctant to pay top dollar to attend shows devoted largely to newer material. building a concert around a classic album is a way for an artist to let the audience know it's safe to come back -- the show will be full of fan-friendly favorites.  david muha  basking ridge, n.j.  performing albums live
