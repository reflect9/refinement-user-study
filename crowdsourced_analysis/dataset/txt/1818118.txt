world briefing | africa: ivory coast: u.n. extends mandate of troops   the security council voted to keep a 12,000-strong force of united nations and french peacekeepers another six months to help disarm militias, prepare for elections and monitor a buffer zone and fragile cease-fire between the government-run south and rebel-held north. the council has twice extended the term of president laurent gbagbo, which ran out in 2005, in anticipation of elections but has authorized a political opponent, charles konan banny, the prime minister, to organize the vote, an arrangement that mr. gbagbo has challenged.  warren hoge
