italian by birth, german in style   the germanic leitmotif found throughout alto adige (a k a south tyrol) emerges in tiefenbrunner's stylish 2005 pinot bianco, also labeled weissburgunder.  lean, tangy, awash in tropical nuances even though it comes from northernmost italy, this austere, lingering white distantly resembles mineral-rich rieslings from austria. it can accompany asparagus (a difficult match for wine), risotto, crustaceans and pork.  eli zabar's w.i.n.e., 1415 third avenue (80th street), charges $18.99.  howard g. goldberg  wine under $20
